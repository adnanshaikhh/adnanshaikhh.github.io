Diffie-Hellman Key Exchange

The Diffe-Hellman key exchange was one of the first public-key protocols that aimed at public key exchange over an insecure channel.

A basic analogy based on colored paints works like this,

Original Implementation

  1. Pick integers g and p, such that p is prime
  2. Alice chooses a private integer a and sends over A = g^a mod p
  3. Bob chooses a private integer b and sends over B = g^b mod p
  4. Alice computes B^a mod p
  5. Bob computes A^b mod p
  6. They both arrive at the same number.

This works since B^a mod p = g^b^a mod p => g^a^b mod p = A^b mod p

A prime number p of at least 600 digits would be intractable even by the fastest modern computers today. Given A, g and p, finding a is known as the discrete logarithm problem. And g^a mod p is known as modular exponentiaion and can be done very efficiently.
